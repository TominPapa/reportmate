Analysis
Wins & Concerns
✅ What Went Well
Blog content drove 31% traffic surge
4 new articles published in March collectively contributed 1,840 incremental sessions. The /blog section now accounts for 38% of all organic traffic, up from 29% last month.
GA4 · /blog sessions +31.2%
2 target keywords entered Top 10
"best moisturizer for dry skin" improved from position 9.3 to 4.8. "hydrating serum review" moved from 14.1 to 7.2.
GSC · Keyword positions
Engagement quality improved
Average session duration increased 8.8% to 2m 41s, suggesting content relevance is improving alongside traffic volume.
GA4 · Avg. session duration
⚠️ Areas to Watch
Conversions declined despite traffic growth
310 conversions vs 326 in February (-4.9%). Traffic volume is up but purchase completions dropped slightly.
Possible explanation: New blog traffic may be earlier-stage visitors (informational intent), not yet ready to purchase. This is an inference, not confirmed.
GA4 · Conversions -4.9%
Top landing page CTR below site average
The /collections/serums page receives strong impressions (12,400/month) but CTR of 3.1% is below the site average of 5.4%, suggesting a meta description optimization opportunity.
GSC · Page CTR report

Recommendations
April Action Plan
  • 1
    Publish 3 bottom-of-funnel articles targeting buying keywords High Priority
    Current traffic gains are weighted toward informational content. To recover conversion rate, we recommend publishing 3 product-comparison or "best X for Y" articles this month that target users with purchase intent. Target keywords identified in GSC query report.
  • 2
    Optimize meta description for /collections/serums Medium Priority
    This page has strong impression volume but below-average CTR (3.1% vs site avg 5.4%). Rewriting the meta description to include a clear value proposition and action phrase could improve click-through rate by an estimated 1–2 percentage points.
  • 3
    Monitor newly ranked keywords weekly Medium Priority
    The two keywords that entered Top 10 this month are in positions 4.8 and 7.2 — prime territory for ranking volatility. We will track these weekly and respond quickly to any position drops with internal linking or content refreshes.
  • 4
    Review conversion funnel for blog-sourced traffic Low Priority
    To better understand the conversion gap, we'll set up a GA4 funnel exploration comparing conversion rates by traffic source. This will confirm whether new blog visitors are converting at a lower rate and help us design appropriate CTAs for informational content pages.
Next Report
April 2026 performance report will be delivered by May 5, 2026. We will specifically track conversion rate recovery and keyword position stability as follow-ups to this month's action items.
