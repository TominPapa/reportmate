Executive Summary
NovaBrew Coffee Co. · March 2026
⚠ Traffic Decline Alert

March was a difficult month for NovaBrew's organic channel. A significant drop in sessions and conversions was recorded across all major landing pages. This report analyzes probable causes and outlines a recovery plan.

What the Data Shows
Overall Performance
Organic sessions fell from 18,420 → 14,317 ↓ −22.3% vs Feb over the 31-day period. This is the steepest single-month decline recorded in the past 12 months.
Conversion Impact
Goal completions from organic dropped from 482 → 332 ↓ −31.1% vs Feb. The decline outpaces traffic loss, suggesting landing page quality was also affected.
Visibility (Google Search Console)
Impressions fell from 312,000 → 247,800 ↓ −20.6% and average position worsened from 18.2 → 21.6 ↓ −3.4 positions.
One Positive Signal
Blog content published in Q4 2025 continues to generate clicks. "Best Home Espresso Machines" maintained a stable Position 8.4 throughout March, suggesting evergreen content is holding.
Our Assessment
📉 Probable Cause: Algorithm Update + Page Speed

Based on the data pattern — broad impression loss across multiple pages simultaneously — this decline is consistent with an algorithm sensitivity event rather than a single page issue. Google's March 2026 core update (rolled out Mar 5–18) affected content quality signals. Additionally, Core Web Vitals scores for the /shop and /collections pages deteriorated in February, which may have amplified the impact. Note: This is an inference from traffic patterns; direct algorithm cause cannot be confirmed from CSV data alone.

Cause Analysis
NovaBrew Coffee Co. · March 2026
Most Affected Pages
🔴 /shop — Lost 8.7 Positions
The main shop page dropped from position 14.1 to 22.8 in GSC. This page had Core Web Vitals warnings flagged in February (LCP > 4.2s). The combination of thin product descriptions and slow load time aligns with E-E-A-T quality signals that were emphasized in the March core update.
🔴 /collections/single-origin — Lost 12.4 Positions
This collection page fell from position 19.3 to 31.7. It has zero internal links pointing to it, making it an orphan page. 0 internal links detected in the crawl data. Crawl frequency may have dropped accordingly.
🟡 /blog/* — Moderate Position Loss
Blog posts collectively lost an average of 2.1 positions. Posts older than 18 months are showing the most decay. Content freshness signals may need updating for posts targeting competitive keywords like "best coffee subscription."
🟡 Bounce Rate Increase on /shop (+12pp)
Despite lower traffic, bounce rate rose from 48% to 60% on the shop page. This suggests users arriving from search are not finding what they expect — a potential title/meta description mismatch with actual page content.
What's Still Working
✅ Evergreen Blog Content is Resilient

The /blog/espresso-guide page remained stable at position 8.4–8.6 throughout March. This page was last updated in January 2026 with fresh statistics and new images. Maintained position during algorithm update — confirming that freshness + depth is working for this content type.

✅ CTR Held Steady Despite Ranking Losses

Despite losing impressions and rankings, click-through rate only declined marginally from 3.15% → 3.07% CTR −0.08pp. This suggests title tags and meta descriptions remain compelling — the issue is Google showing our pages to fewer users, not users ignoring us when we do appear.

Priority Actions

    Fix Core Web Vitals on /shop (LCP > 4.2s → target < 2.5s)

    This page is currently an orphan with zero internal links. Add contextual links from 3–5 blog posts and from the main /shop navigation. This should help Google rediscover and re-crawl the page within 2–3 weeks.
    🔴 Urgent — Week 1 On-Page SEO
  • 3
    Refresh 3 Blog Posts Older than 18 Months
    Update statistics, add 2026 data points, expand word count on posts targeting "best coffee subscription," "cold brew at home," and "espresso vs americano." These posts are showing position decay that typically responds to freshness updates.
