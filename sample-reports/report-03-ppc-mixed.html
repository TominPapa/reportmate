Executive Summary
FitCore Studio · March 2026
⚖ What "Mixed Signals" Means for FitCore

On the surface, March looks fine — ROAS is nearly identical to February (3.8× vs 3.9×). But underneath, spend increased by $2,320 (+18.6%) while the number of conversions only grew by 4.2%. The platform-level picture is even more divided: Google Ads is under pressure, while Meta Ads delivered its best month in Q1.

The Hidden Problem
Google Ads CPC rose from $1.84 → $2.18 ↑ +18.5% MoM — likely driven by increased competition in "home gym equipment" keywords during March (tax refund season). If this trend continues into April, Google ROAS will fall below 3× without budget adjustments.
The Bright Spot
Meta Ads delivered a 4.6× ROAS in March — up from 4.1× in February. The new "Home Workout Challenge" video creative posted March 8 is responsible for the majority of this gain, generating 214 conversions at a $1.9% conversion rate.
Revenue vs Spend
Total ad spend: $14,820 ↑ +$2,320 vs Feb. Total attributed revenue: $56,316 ↑ +$4,100 vs Feb. Net ROAS held at 3.8× because revenue grew proportionally — but the spend buffer is thinning.
Our Recommendation
Shift $1,500–$2,000 of April budget from Google Ads into Meta Ads (video creatives) while CPC normalizes. Do not pause Google — maintain brand keyword coverage. Investigate Google Shopping as a lower-CPC alternative for product terms.
Key Question for April
🤔 Is the Google CPC Spike Seasonal or Structural?

March–April sees elevated CPC in fitness-related keywords historically (New Year resolution tail + spring body prep). If this is seasonal, CPC should normalize by May and the current Google strategy is sound. If competitors have permanently increased bids or new advertisers entered the auction, a strategic keyword audit will be needed. We recommend pulling a 90-day CPC trend in the April review to confirm.

Signal Analysis
FitCore Studio · March 2026
Google Ads — Warning Signs
⚠ CPC Rising Faster Than Conversions

Google Ads CPC increased from $1.84 to $2.18 +18.5% CPC MoM. Spend increased by $1,980 but only generated 12 additional conversions +3.8% conversions on +25% spend. This widening gap is the clearest warning signal in this month's data. If spend grows faster than conversions, ROAS will continue declining even if revenue appears to grow.

🟡 "Home Gym Equipment" Keywords Under Auction Pressure

Average position for core non-brand keywords declined from 2.4 to 3.1 position drop −0.7. This is consistent with competitors raising bids. March is historically competitive in the fitness vertical (tax refund stimulus + pre-summer body prep). This is an inference from position and CPC patterns; auction-level data not available in CSV.

Meta Ads — What's Working
✅ Video Creative "Home Workout Challenge" is Outperforming

The video ad launched March 8 generated 214 conversions — 78% of all Meta conversions this month — at a CVR of 1.9%. Thumb-stop rate is estimated above average based on the CPM holding flat CPM stable at $11.40 despite increased impressions. This creative should be extended into April with fresh hooks (first 3 seconds varied) to prevent fatigue.

✅ Retargeting Audience Efficiency Improved

Website retargeting audience (30-day visitors) showed a 2.8× higher CVR vs cold prospecting audiences in March. Budget allocated to retargeting increased from 22% → 31% of Meta spend, contributing to the overall ROAS improvement. Consider expanding retargeting window to 60 days to grow the addressable audience.

Should We Rebalance Budget?
✅ Arguments FOR shifting budget to Meta
  • 💡 Meta ROAS is 4.64× vs Google's 3.15× this month
  • 💡 Meta CPA is ~$18 vs Google's ~$30 this month
  • 💡 Winning creative identified — can scale
  • 💡 Google CPC rising with no sign of easing
⚠ Arguments AGAINST a full shift
  • ⚠ Google captures high-intent purchase searches
  • ⚠ Meta conversion data may include view-through (not all cash)
  • ⚠ Brand keyword pause risks competitor ad takeover
  • ⚠ CPC spike may be seasonal — check May before restructuring

    The "Home Workout Challenge" video will begin showing fatigue by Week 3 of April. Prepare 3 variants with different opening 3 seconds (hook A: question, hook B: transformation result, hook C: social proof). Keep the body identical — only vary the hook to identify what drives the click.
    🔴 Urgent — April 7 Creative Meta

    Audit Google Keyword CPC — Pause Low-ROAS Non-Brand Terms
    Run a keyword-level ROAS report in Google Ads. Identify any non-brand keywords with CPC above $2.50 and ROAS below 2.5×. Pause or significantly reduce bids on these terms. Expected spend savings: $400–$700/month without meaningful revenue impact.

  • 4
    Expand Meta Retargeting Window: 30 Days → 60 Days
    The 30-day retargeting audience drove 2.8× better CVR than prospecting. Expanding to 60 days will increase the addressable audience size by approximately 40% based on typical site traffic patterns. Monitor frequency cap to ensure users aren't seeing the same ad more than 4× per week.
    🟡 Priority — April 3 Meta Audience
  • 5
    Test Google Shopping Campaigns for Product Keywords
    Google Shopping typically delivers 15–25% lower CPC than Search for product-specific queries. Allocate $500 test budget to Shopping for top 5 SKUs. If CPA is below $20, scale to replace search spend on product terms. Review after 3 weeks.
    📋 Test — April Week 2 Google Budget
